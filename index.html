ABOUT US

Pottery is the art of shaping clay into objects such as pots, vases, bowls, and sculptures, which are then hardened by firing. It is both functional, used for storage, cooking, and carrying water, and decorative, serving as a form of artistic expression.

The main types of pottery are earthenware, which is porous and fired at low temperatures, stoneware, which is durable and fired at higher temperatures, and porcelain, which is smooth, fine, and highly durable. Pottery is one of the oldest human crafts, dating back to around 29,000 BC. It has been discovered in ancient civilizations such as China, Japan, Mesopotamia, Greece, Egypt, and various regions of Africa. Across these cultures, it played an important role in daily life, trade, rituals, and art. Its significance goes beyond practical use; pottery provides valuable insight into ancient traditions and cultural development. Even today, it remains a vital form of art, cultural heritage, and modern design.
